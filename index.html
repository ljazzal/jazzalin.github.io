Loïc James Azzalini

Robotics student | University of Toronto Institute for Aerospace Studies (UTIAS)

As a MASc student at UTIAS, I conduct research at the intersection of robotics and computational neuroscience. I am advised by Professor G.M.T. D’Eleuterio and collaborate closely with the Neural System and Brain Signal Processing Lab (NSBSPL) at the Krembil Research Institute.

My background is in Mechatronics Engineering and AI at the University of Waterloo. Over the course of my undergrad co-op program, I had the opportunity to get hands-on experience in automation and robotics, including as a research intern at the ABB Corporate Research Center in Switzerland and as a software developer at Clearpath Robotics.

Research interests

I am particularly interested in the combination of robust state estimation and control algorithms with neural oscillatory dynamics. As of June 2021, I have had the opportunity of working with SpiNNaker neuromorphic hardware to validate neuronal network models and explore strategies for closed-loop control. In the future, I hope to integrate my findings into a larger robotic system to evaluate the influence of the environment on neuronal dynamics.

In general, I am enthusiastic about the interplay between robotics and neuroscience: on the one hand as a source of inspiration to design and ultimately better understand robotic systems, on the other as an opportunity to embody abstract models.
